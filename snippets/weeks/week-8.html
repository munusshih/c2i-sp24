  • Generative Pattern

    A pattern is a repeated decorative design: each element in a pattern repeats in a predictable manner. However, what if we were to introduce randomness into the fold? Your task is to create a generative pattern, where each element has recognizable shared characteristics but is also uniquely different in some way.

    Consider which characteristics are your constants and variants. For instance, do you have a limited color palette? Do each of your elements have the same width but a random border-radius? Do you want the arrangement of the elements (read: position) to be left up to chance or be static and gridded? Create at least 10 elements that serve as a basis for your pattern. The elements can be shapes or pieces of text.

    As always, create a new subfolder called exercise-5 within your username.github.io folder. Create a new index.html, styles.css, and script.js file within that folder to work in.

    To get full credit, make sure you hit all the requirements in the checklist below.

    2. This exercise is linked out from my class homepage.

    3. My pattern consists of at least 10 elements.

    4. I’ve considered the constants and variants of my pattern. The elements share some characteristics but are also uniquely different.

    5. I utilized Math.random() to generate the variant characteristics in my pattern.
